						Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist

Do’s

1. Maintain a clean and professional profile  
   Keep your bio, profile picture, and posts appropriate, as they can be viewed by professors, recruiters, or others.

2. Think before you post  
   Always consider how your post might be perceived by others. Once something is online, it is difficult to remove completely.

3. Use privacy settings wisely  
   Regularly check and update your account privacy settings to control who can see your content and personal information.

4. Share meaningful and positive content  
   Try to post content that reflects your interests, skills, or achievements instead of random or negative posts.

5. Communicate respectfully  
   Be polite and respectful in comments and messages, even if you disagree with someone’s opinion.


Don’ts

1. Do not share sensitive personal information  
   Avoid posting your phone number, address, passwords, or financial details on any platform.

2. Avoid posting inappropriate or offensive content  
   Content that is abusive, discriminatory, or vulgar can harm your reputation and future opportunities.

3. Do not engage in online arguments or fights  
   Getting into unnecessary conflicts online can create a negative image and lead to stress.

4. Avoid spreading unverified information  
   Do not forward or share news without checking its authenticity, as it can spread misinformation.

5. Do not overuse social media during important work  
   Excessive use of social media can affect your productivity and academic performance.
